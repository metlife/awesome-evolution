## Primate Research Papers

- **[Constructing primate phylogenies from ancient retrovirus sequences](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.96.18.10254)** - 1999. Johnson and Coffin. This is the seminal paper testing the hypothesis that ERVs can be used to reconstruct primate phylogenies. The authors used a set of 12 ERV loci to construct a phylogeny of 10 primate species, and found that the resulting tree was consistent with the known phylogeny of these species.

- **[Origin and Deep Evolution of Human Endogenous Retroviruses in Pan-Primates](https://www.mdpi.com/1999-4915/14/7/1370/)** - 2022. Zhang and Cui. This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the origin and evolution of human endogenous retroviruses (HERVs) in primates. The authors used a combination of phylogenetic and genomic analyses to trace the evolutionary history of HERVs in primates, and found that HERVs have been present in primate genomes for at least 40 million years.

- **[The diversity and evolution of retroviruses: Perspectives from viral “fossils”](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8922424/)** - 2022. Zheng, Wei, Cui. This paper provides a comprehensive review of the diversity and evolution of retroviruses, with a focus on the insights gained from the study of endogenous retroviruses (ERVs). The authors discuss the various mechanisms by which retroviruses have evolved, including recombination, mutation, and selection, and highlight the importance of ERVs as "fossils" that can provide valuable information about the evolutionary history of retroviruses.

- **[Journal Viruses: Special Issue](https://www.mdpi.com/journal/viruses/special_issues/endogenous_retrovirus/)** - 2022. This special issue of the journal Viruses is dedicated to the study of endogenous retroviruses (ERVs). It includes a collection of research articles and reviews that cover various aspects of ERV biology, including their evolution, function, and impact on host genomes.

## Vertebrate Research Papers

- **[Koala retrovirus: a genome invasion in real time](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/gb-2006-7-11-241/)** - 2006. Stoye. This paper provides an overview of the discovery and characterization of the koala retrovirus (KoRV), which is currently invading the koala genome. The author discusses the implications of this invasion for koala health and conservation, as well as the potential for KoRV to serve as a model for studying retroviral evolution and host-virus interactions.

- **[Diversity and transmission of koala retrovirus: a case study in three captive koala populations](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-022-18939-6/)** - 2022. Joyce, et al. This paper provides a detailed analysis of the diversity and transmission of koala retrovirus (KoRV) in three captive koala populations. The authors used a combination of genomic and epidemiological analyses to investigate the prevalence and diversity of KoRV in these populations, and found that KoRV is highly diverse and can be transmitted both vertically and horizontally.
